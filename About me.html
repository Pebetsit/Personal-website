About me

I'm Pebetsi Thokwane, a final-year PhD candidate in Physics at the University of South Africa, specializing in renewable energy technology. My research centers on the use of upconversion nanoparticles to enhance the absorption efficiency of perovskite solar cells—specifically methylammonium lead iodide (MAPbI₃)—with the aim of improving next-generation solar energy solutions. I hold a Master’s degree in Physics from UNISA (2023), and previously earned my BSc in Physics and Chemistry and Honours in Physics from the University of Venda in 2020 and 2021, respectively.

My academic and research background has laid a strong foundation in analytical thinking, data interpretation, and scientific communication. This naturally led me to expand into data analytics, where I apply tools like Python, R, SQL, Power BI, and Tableau to extract insights, visualize patterns, and solve complex problems. I’ve completed certifications from institutions such as Coursera, and WorldQuant University, strengthening my technical toolkit across data science domains. In my current role as a Laboratory Assistant at UNISA, I support experimental research, mentor students in analytical techniques, and maintain data quality and instrumentation standards. I also bring prior experience from the University of Venda, where I worked as a Laboratory Demonstrator. Additionally, I am currently expanding my skill set into full-stack web development, combining front-end and back-end technologies to build functional, data-driven applications.

With a multidisciplinary approach that blends renewable energy research, data analytics, and web development, I’m committed to continuous learning and applying technology to real-world challenges.
