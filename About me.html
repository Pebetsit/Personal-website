About Me

I am Pebetsi Thokwane, a Physics PhD candidate at the University of South Africa. My thesis has been completed and is awaiting the formal submission and examination process.

My research focuses on lanthanide-doped upconversion nanomaterials and their application in improving light absorption in perovskite solar-cell materials. This work has strengthened my ability to approach complex problems with scientific rigour and careful analysis.

I hold an MSc in Physics from UNISA, a BSc Honours in Physics, and a BSc in Physics and Chemistry from the University of Venda. My academic background has given me a strong foundation in research, technical writing, and problem solving.

I have experience in scientific research, laboratory work, undergraduate practical instruction, data collection, data analysis, troubleshooting, and solving complex problems. I have worked with analytical instruments including XRD, UV-Visible spectroscopy, photoluminescence, Raman, FTIR, thermoluminescence, TGA, SEM sample preparation, and TEM.

I have published research papers and completed my PhD thesis. These experiences have helped me develop strong technical communication skills and a disciplined approach to scientific investigation.

I am developing software skills through practical projects and self-directed learning. My current software and technical skills include HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript fundamentals, Python, Git, GitHub, responsive web design, scientific data analysis, and problem solving.

I am applying for graduate software engineering opportunities and hope to combine my analytical Physics background with software development, continuing to grow through practical projects, structured learning, and collaborative engineering work.
